HISTORY

The history of restaurants dates back to ancient times, with evidence of public eateries in ancient Rome and China. The first modern restaurant, as we know it today, appeared in Paris in 1765, when a man named Boulanger served "restaurants" (restorative broths). By the late 18th century, more formal dining establishments emerged in Paris, leading to the French Revolution spreading the concept. The 19th century saw the rise of luxury restaurants, like Delmonico's in New York. The 20th century introduced fast food with White Castle in 1921. Chains like McDonald's revolutionized dining in the 1950s. Today, restaurants range from fine dining to fast-casual worldwide.
